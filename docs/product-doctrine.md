# Product Doctrine

Constellation is a relational astrology app. It is not a compatibility scorer.

## Core Frame

> No scores. No verdicts. Just the pattern between you.

The app helps users understand what different people activate in them, what they activate in others, and what kind of relationship field appears between them.

## Principles

1. **Not compatibility. Dynamics.**
   The app does not rank or judge relationships. It describes activation, similarity, difference, friction, regulation, pacing, repair, and relational field patterns.

2. **No fatalism.**
   Astrology describes symbolic pattern and relational weather. It does not override lived behavior, consent, accountability, or choice.

3. **Difference is not threat.**
   The app should translate difference rather than pathologize it.

4. **Strong contact is not the same as healthy contact.**
   Intensity, chemistry, significance, safety, stability, and repair capacity are separate categories.

5. **Synastry and composite are distinct layers.**
   Synastry describes how two people affect each other. Composite describes the relationship as its own living field.

6. **Every hard dynamic needs a repair path.**
   The app should not leave users with “this is hard.” It should translate tension into a loop and offer a practical repair principle.

7. **Relationship type changes weighting.**
   The same contact means different things in a romantic relationship, parent-child dynamic, sibling bond, friendship, collaboration, mentor relationship, or admired-figure resonance.

8. **Synthesize before explaining.**
   Reports should begin with a bird’s-eye thesis before listing technical evidence.

9. **Exactness overrides volume.**
   Tight contacts, angle contacts, luminary contacts, and exact composite signatures carry more interpretive weight than many loose or sign-only contacts.

10. **The map cannot override lived reality.**
    The app can describe the field. It cannot decide whether a relationship is safe, viable, or worth continuing.

## Voice

The interpretive language should be:

- somatic
- relational
- practical
- emotionally legible
- non-fatalistic
- psychologically nuanced
- capable of depth without fearmongering

## Preferred Phrases

- “This activates…”
- “The relationship field may…”
- “This can feel like…”
- “The repair question is…”
- “Recognition is not regulation.”
- “Bonding is not the same as safety.”
- “Intensity requires repair capacity.”
- “The surface of the bond and the engine of the bond are not always the same.”

## Avoid

- compatibility scores
- “good match” / “bad match”
- deterministic soulmate claims
- predicting abuse, divorce, or trauma
- using stable/unstable language without context
- moralizing difficult signatures
- treating soft aspects as automatically healthy
