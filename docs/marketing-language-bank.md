# Constellation Marketing Language Bank

This document collects brand, positioning, and marketing language for future use.

## Core Tagline

**An observatory for all your human relationships.**

Why it works:

- Preserves the Observatory lineage.
- Makes the product broader than romance.
- Opens space for partners, exes, family, friends, collaborators, mentors, clients, and admired figures.
- Frames the product as an ongoing place of observation rather than a one-time compatibility calculator.
- Supports future features: saved relationships, timing, journaling, AI chat, divination, and human-work bridges.

## Strong Variants

- An observatory for the relationships that shape your life.
- A living observatory for the people who shape your life.
- Map the patterns, timing, and meaning of your human relationships.
- A relational observatory for the people, patterns, and bonds that shape your life.
- Understand what each relationship activates in you.
- See the field between you more clearly.
- Track the patterns moving through your relationships.
- A map of attraction, friction, timing, and repair.

## Positioning Statements

Constellation is not a compatibility-score app.

It is a relational observatory: a place to map, understand, and revisit the patterns between you and the people who shape your life.

Constellation helps you explore:

- what pulls you together
- what gets activated
- what feels easy or familiar
- what becomes intense or difficult
- what belongs to each person individually
- what belongs to the relationship itself
- what kind of repair, reflection, or response may be possible

## Copy Principles

The language should feel:

- intimate but not invasive
- poetic but clear
- psychologically useful without pretending to be therapy
- astrologically sophisticated without becoming jargon-heavy
- non-fatalistic
- relational and somatic
- capable of depth without becoming melodramatic

Avoid:

- soulmate bait
- compatibility scoring language
- deterministic predictions
- fear-based red-flag marketing
- overpromising emotional certainty
- implying the app can replace lived judgment, therapy, or direct communication
