# Composite Doctrine

The composite chart is a synthesis layer. It describes the relationship field that forms between two people; it is not a second synastry and should not duplicate synastry aspect-by-aspect.

## Use composite evidence to confirm and amplify

Composite themes are strongest when they echo already-detected synastry, overlay, or motif evidence. A composite concentration can support convergence and help name the shared field, but it should not erase the interpersonal dynamics shown by synastry.

## Handle composite-only claims carefully

Composite-only signatures should be framed as hypotheses about the relationship atmosphere, not as definitive claims about either person or the outcome of the bond. Avoid building the report around an isolated composite placement unless context and other evidence support it.

## Avoid filler

Do not pad reports with generic “composite Sun,” “composite Moon,” or “composite Ascendant” baseline language. Use composite placements when they sharpen the central story, explain a repeated motif, or clarify a structural growth edge.

## Outer-planet and Saturn composites

- Composite Saturn describes structure, duty, limits, timing, and the work required to make the bond reliable.
- Composite Pluto describes intensity, power, depth, and transformation pressure.
- Composite Uranus describes freedom needs, disruption, experimentation, and unstable rhythm.
- Composite Neptune describes longing, idealization, porousness, inspiration, and the need for reality checks.

Frame these as structural or growth-edge themes, not verdicts.
