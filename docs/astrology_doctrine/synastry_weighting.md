# Synastry Weighting Doctrine

Synastry weighting ranks relationship signatures for narrative priority. It does not grade compatibility, predict outcome, or decide whether a bond is healthy.

## Tier 1 — report-leading signatures

These can usually open a report when supported by clean evidence and appropriate context.

- Sun/Moon contacts.
- Moon/Moon contacts.
- Venus/Mars contacts.
- Sun, Moon, or Venus contacts to the Ascendant/Descendant axis.
- 7th-house ruler and Ascendant-ruler cross contacts when birth-time support and implementation evidence are present.
- Saturn contacts to personal planets, especially Moon, Venus, Sun, or angles.

## Tier 2 — major relationship signatures

These are central themes, especially when repeated or convergent, but should not automatically outrank Tier 1 recognition, attachment, or structure signatures.

- Venus/Pluto and Mars/Pluto intensity.
- Moon/Venus and Moon/Mars emotional-affectional or emotional-reactive chemistry.
- Nodal contacts, framed non-fatalistically as familiarity, repetition, or developmental pull.
- 1st, 4th, 5th, 7th, and 8th house overlays.
- Major composite echoes or concentrations that repeat synastry themes.
- Venus/Uranus, Venus/Neptune, Moon/Saturn, and Moon/Uranus.

## Tier 3 — supporting signatures

These add texture and specificity but usually should not lead a general relationship report.

- Mercury contacts.
- Jupiter contacts.
- Mars/Mars contacts.
- Secondary house overlays.
- 10th-house or MC contacts unless the question concerns public life, career, vocation, visibility, collaboration, or reputation.

## Tier 4 — contextual and advanced signatures

These require restraint and should usually remain supporting or omitted.

- Minor aspects.
- Wide or isolated contacts.
- Unsupported asteroid material.
- Advanced points.

## Ranking principles

- **Orb tightness matters.** Exact or close contacts speak louder than wide contacts of the same type.
- **Convergence matters.** Repetition across synastry, overlays, composite, and natal echoes can promote a theme.
- **Repetition can promote a theme.** Several Tier 2 or Tier 3 signatures in the same motif may outrank an isolated higher-tier detail in practice.
- **Priority is not moral meaning.** Weighting answers “what should the report address first?” not “is this relationship good?”
