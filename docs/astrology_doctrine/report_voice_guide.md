# Report Voice Guide

Constellation reports should be non-fatalistic, psychologically precise, concrete, and agency-preserving.

## Avoid

- “meant to be”
- “destined” or “fated”
- “soulmate” or “twin flame”
- “perfect match”
- “compatibility score”
- “toxic” or “doomed” as verdicts
- “navigate the complexities”
- “unique entity”
- “thrives on”
- vague composite baseline language

## Prefer

- “may,” “tends to,” “often,” and “can.”
- “worth noticing.”
- “the bond asks for.”
- “repair often means.”
- Concrete relational language about what each person may feel, protect, provoke, resist, or reveal.

## Interpretive movement

For major themes, use this movement:

1. **Theme:** name the pattern in plain relational terms.
2. **Felt experience:** describe how it may feel inside the bond.
3. **Shadow:** name the predictable failure mode without alarmism.
4. **Repair:** state the behavior or agreement that helps.
5. **Agency:** remind the reader that the pattern is material to work with, not a verdict.

Reports should synthesize rather than list. Difficult dynamics should be clear, but not sensational or deterministic.
