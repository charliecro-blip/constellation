## Part 1 — New Aspect-Pair Interpretation Doctrine (Sargent)

Sargent provides the most granular aspect-pair prose of any source surveyed. Primary value: nuanced valence language for Mercury, Venus, Mars, Jupiter, and Saturn cross-aspects — beyond what Arroyo or Davison provide at this level of specificity.

### Mercury aspects

**Mercury–Mercury:**
[SOURCE: Sargent p. 70] Harmonious aspects (conjunction, sextile, trine): similar reasoning processes, agreement in principle, enlivening conversational give-and-take. Mercury square Mercury: misunderstandings, serious differences of opinion, discussions end in impasse; "not good for marriage, for it does not promote companionship." Fixed-sign Mercury square adds obstinacy.

[SYNTHESIS] Mercury square Mercury is the only purely intellectual aspect on Eddington's Worst list (as Mercury opposite Mercury — treated as equivalent in severity). For Constellation, Mercury conflicts deserve penalty weighting in friendship/communication pattern layer, not just in romantic-axis scoring.

**Mercury–Venus (harmonious):**
[SOURCE: Sargent p. 71] Brings out best in intellectual and artistic interests; mutual understanding through sympathy and affection; "very good in marriage or in parent-child charts." Mercury–Venus hard aspects: "not seriously conflicting but not completely harmonious" — Mercury tends to disregard emotional needs of Venus, seems cold and calculating.

**Mercury–Mars:**
[SOURCE: Sargent pp. 71–72] Conjunction: enlivening but potentially irritating; trine/sextile: stimulating without irritation. Square/opposition: conflict between aggressive Mars spirit and Mercury reasoning — "arguments, quarrels, misunderstandings."

**Mercury–Saturn:**
[SOURCE: Sargent pp. 72–73] Harmonious aspects: Saturn stabilizes Mercury, offers experience and knowledge; good for teacher-student, mentor relationships. Adverse: Saturn "very critical, exacting, disapproving, discouraging" of Mercury's mental attainments; causes resentment and self-doubt.

---

### Venus aspects

**Venus–Venus:**
[SOURCE: Sargent p. 75] Harmonious: many mutual interests, similar attitudes in love, social life, affections; "can do much to smooth out rough places caused by adverse aspects." Opposition/square: "minor discords... affection not expressed in the same way... bit difficult in marriage."

[SYNTHESIS] Venus–Venus conjunction is a pure background harmonic — it doesn't generate romantic pull on its own but reduces friction from other hard aspects. Treat similarly to Moon–Moon trine: comfort amplifier.

**Venus–Mars:**
[SOURCE: Sargent pp. 75–76] Conjunction: "strong aspect of attraction between the sexes... Mars sometimes tends to be possessive, jealous, or impetuous... Venus has a soothing effect on Mars." Trine/sextile: same without emotional excesses. Opposition/square: similar attraction but "more disturbing emotional reactions... jealousy will often arise... one will resist the other."

[SYNTHESIS] Venus–Mars square/opposition is NOT a dealbreaker — it describes attraction with tension, not absence of attraction. Language should acknowledge the pull before naming the friction. "There's a charge here that can feel exciting and destabilizing in the same breath."

**Venus–Jupiter:**
[SOURCE: Sargent pp. 76–77] All aspects (including opposition): mutual benefit, enjoyment of same pastimes, optimism, generosity. Conjunction/opposition may stimulate extravagance or overindulgence. Square: "boredom" and insincerity rather than warm generosity.

**Venus–Saturn:**
[SOURCE: Sargent pp. 77–78] Conjunction: "stabilizes emotions of Venus... Saturn makes marriage binding... aspect supports endurability... Saturn feeds on affections that Venus offers." But: Saturn risks being too demanding; Venus risks depression. Trine/sextile: structuring without burden. Opposition/square: "Saturn will prove demanding, restricting, selfish, condemning, resentful, critical, disapproving, and jealous... many unhappy experiences... Venus would find it difficult to give affection to Saturn."

[SYNTHESIS] Venus–Saturn conjunction is Sargent's primary endurance indicator — appearing in long-lasting bonds regardless of happiness. The app should distinguish between binding and bonding. "This contact can create a lasting structure — the question is whether it's one both people want to inhabit."

**Venus–Uranus:**
[SOURCE: Sargent pp. 78–79] Conjunction: "very magnetic attraction... romance usually starts as an infatuation... something unusual or unconventional in this association... unless many aspects to indicate loyalty and steadfastness, romance may be short-lived." Trine/sextile: stimulating, encourages creative ability. Opposition/square: exciting but "uncertain, unpredictable, undependable... not recommended for marriage."

**Venus–Neptune:**
[SOURCE: Sargent pp. 79–80] Conjunction: "sympathy... deep affection and devotion... Neptune tends to entice or bewitch Venus... note of pity into the attraction. Pity is akin to love, but not always a fortunate beginning." Hard aspects: "confusion, deception, evasion... Neptune can mislead or confuse Venus even though his intentions may be honorable."

[SYNTHESIS] Venus–Neptune is idealization energy — beautiful and fragile. Language should convey the possibility of genuine devotion AND the risk of disillusionment without choosing between them. Not a warning, but an invitation to stay awake.

---

### Mars aspects

**Mars–Mars:**
[SOURCE: Sargent p. 81] Trine/sextile: "unity of desire, energy, action... helpful in marriage for sexual compatibility." Conjunction: similar quality of energy — good if cooperative, but "rivalry, since both may want to take the initiative." Opposition/square: "conflict of individual wills... friction or hostility... vindictiveness."

[SOURCE: Eddington Worst, pp. 129–142] Mars opposite Mars is ranked as one of the 10 worst synastry aspects. "Two partners will seem to be locked in combat a great deal of the time." Water-earth oppositions (Pisces/Virgo, Scorpio/Taurus, Cancer/Capricorn) appear particularly vulnerable. However: Reagan and Reagan (Mars opposite Mars + stellium mismatch) succeeded because of exceptional 7th and 8th house overlays — showing that a strong overlay architecture can override individual hard aspects.

[SYNTHESIS] Mars opposite Mars scores negatively in pattern layer but does NOT disqualify a relationship — especially when romantic-house overlays are strong. Report language should name the friction without catastrophizing.

**Mars–Saturn:**
[SOURCE: Sargent pp. 83–84] Conjunction: Saturn puts a brake on Mars but can teach constructive patience; "in marriage the aspect is a point in favor of endurance. Saturn always binds." Mars stimulates confidence in Saturn. Opposition: Saturn opposes Mars initiative; Mars becomes "rebellious and defiant." Square: "Saturn frustrates Mars, making the latter impatient, angry, and resistant."

[SYNTHESIS] Mars–Saturn conjunction is a friction-plus-endurance pattern — present in many long-lasting but difficult relationships. Not a red flag, but a durability marker with cost. Language: "There's a persistent rub here that can either temper both of you or wear one of you down."

---

### Jupiter and Saturn cross-aspects

**Jupiter–Jupiter:**
[SOURCE: Sargent p. 87] Harmonious aspects: "similarity if not complete agreement in ideals, ethics, aspirations, religious or spiritual concepts... tolerant, considerate, and forgiving toward each other." Opposition/square: differences in moral sentiments and ideals; "not harmfully adverse unless... the comparison chart is extreme."

**Saturn–Saturn:**
[SOURCE: Sargent pp. 90–91] Conjunction: similar problems and responsibilities; "devitalizing effect on each other... increase any tendency to fear, worry, or lack of self-confidence." Trine/sextile: cooperation in responsibility and work; "very good aspect in marriage." Opposition/square: "conflict in attitude toward responsibility and security... discouraging, devitalizing, create serious problems of adjustment."

[SYNTHESIS] Saturn–Saturn conjunction is a subtle negative for romantic synastry — shared anxiety and limitation without compensating warmth. Not dramatic, but worth a small penalty when it's the primary Saturn contact.

## Part 2 — Projection, Shadow, and Anima/Animus Doctrine (Greene)

Greene's Inner Partner chapter provides the most psychologically sophisticated framework available in the source library for explaining why relationships feel fated, why projections collapse post-honeymoon, and what repair actually requires.

### Anima/Animus as the invisible fourth party

[SOURCE: Greene, pp. 123–124] "When animus and anima meet, the animus draws his sword of power and the anima ejects her poison of illusion and seduction." Unconscious activation of inner images is responsible for much of what feels like interpersonal conflict.

[SOURCE: Greene, pp. 123] "There are not merely two people, there are four. The conscious ego of the man, and the conscious ego of the woman, appear to be the participants, and there are the two inner partners as well."

[SYNTHESIS] This is the doctrinal basis for why no aspect analysis is fully sufficient. The report can name what the chart shows but should acknowledge that the inner partner — the unconscious image — shapes how any aspect is actually experienced.

### Projection and "falling in love"

[SOURCE: Greene, pp. 125] Projection is "most certainly involved in 'falling in love' at first sight, though this does not mean that such projections are harmful or negative. On the contrary, they are a necessary catalyst for relationship."

[SOURCE: Greene, pp. 117–118] Post-honeymoon pattern: "the partner has not changed at all, only the projection." The felt experience of a partner "changing" is the projection lifting, not the person changing.

[SYNTHESIS] Report language should implicitly acknowledge this without being reductive. Intensity of early attraction is not evidence of compatibility — it's evidence of projection activation. This supports NOT calling intense Neptune or Pluto synastry "deep connection" without caveats.

### Anima types (relevant to Venus archetype interpretation)

[SOURCE: Greene, pp. 127–128] Wolff's four anima types — Mother (nurturing/possessive), Hetaira (romantic/fickle/culturally refined), Amazon (practical/earthy/sturdy), Medium (visionary/muse/possibly unstable). Each represents what a man unconsciously seeks in a partner.

[SYNTHESIS] Not directly implementable as a discrete pattern, but useful for report voice: when Venus-Neptune or Venus-Uranus is prominent, the "Hetaira/Medium" archetype is being activated. When Venus-Saturn is prominent, the "Amazon/Mother" archetype may dominate. This informs the *kind* of attraction described, not just its intensity.

### Shadow doctrine (Saturn placement as projection location)

[SOURCE: Greene, pp. 111–112] Shadow projections cluster in the sphere of life symbolized by Saturn's house and sign. "It is in this sphere of life that the enemy is objectified and externalised." The intensity of negative reactions to a partner often corresponds to Saturn's position — what the person cannot own in themselves.

[SYNTHESIS] When Person A's Saturn is in a sign that Person B's Sun or Moon occupies, and the aspect is hard (conjunction, opposition, square), the Saturn person is likely to project shadow material onto the Sun/Moon person. They may simultaneously feel bonded to and critical of them. This nuances Saturn-Sun/Moon interpretation: it's not just "responsibility" or "burden" — it's specifically a projection hook.

### Repetitive relationship patterns = anima/animus signal

[SOURCE: Greene, pp. 134–135] "When a disastrous relationship occurs once, we may fool ourselves into believing it is chance; when it occurs twice, it has become a pattern, and a pattern is an unmistakable indication that the anima or animus is at work in the unconscious."

[SYNTHESIS] Do NOT implement: Constellation is not a therapy tool and doesn't have access to relationship history. But this informs the tonal stance — when hard patterns (Venus square Saturn, Moon opposite Pluto) appear repeatedly in a person's natals, the report should encourage reflection rather than blame the partner.

### Integration requirement

[SOURCE: Greene, pp. 118, 124] "The shadow can be realised only through a relation to a partner, and anima and animus only through a relation to a partner of the opposite sex, because only in such a relation do their projections become operative."

[SYNTHESIS] Repair is not about resolving conflict — it's about becoming conscious of the projection. The app's repair-language module should offer this framing implicitly: "The difficulty here may be less about what [Name B] is doing and more about what this relationship is reflecting back to [Name A]."

## Part 3 — Difficult Pattern Language (Eddington Worst)

### The Top 10 Worst Aspects — for Constellation's pattern penalty layer

Eddington Worst's list, distilled for scoring implications:

| Pattern | Eddington ranking | Constellation implication |
|---|---|---|
| Moon opposite Moon | #1 worst | Significant penalty; emotional needs in conflict |
| Mercury opposite Mercury | #2 worst | Communication impasse; especially bad fixed signs |
| Venus square Ascendant | #3 worst | Physical/aesthetic repulsion signal; not relationship-ending but strong misfit |
| Sun conjunct Sun | #4 worst | Scores #49/50 in Jung's marital predictors — friendship pattern, not marriage pattern |
| Stellium with no partner conjunction | #5 worst | Implement as pattern: detect if one person has 3+ personal planets in one sign with no partner conjunction |
| Venus opposite Mercury | #6 worst | Love language vs. communication style mismatch |
| Moon opposite Mercury | #7 worst | Heart vs. head split; "you just don't get me" |
| 12th house overlay | #8 worst | Ambivalent: obsession OR transcendent bond; NOT same as romantic overlay |
| Venus opposite Saturn | #9 worst | Structure without warmth; endurance without love |
| Mars opposite Mars | #10 worst | Combat pattern; water-earth opposition hardest |

[SOURCE: Eddington Worst appendix, p. 144] Jung's "Least Observed Marital Aspects" (least common in long-term marriages): Sun opposite Sun, Sun conjunct Sun, Mars opposite Mars, woman's Sun conjunct man's Venus, Venus conjunct Venus, woman's Mars conjunct man's Sun, woman's Ascendant conjunct man's Sun.

[SYNTHESIS] Several of Jung's "least marital" aspects are currently treated as positives in many synastry tools. Constellation should NOT penalize these aggressively, but should also not inflate them. Venus conjunct Venus = background harmony, not marriage indicator on its own. Sun conjunct Sun = friend signal; downweight for romantic-commitment scoring.

### 12th house overlay — revised doctrine

[SOURCE: Eddington Worst, pp. 116–120] 12th house overlay "can go one of two distinct ways: lifelong love and yearning for the partner, OR obsession (usually by the house person toward the planet person)." The house person is typically more open; the planet person may feel exposed or ill at ease. 12th house overlays → shared dreams, thinking about each other obsessively, possible telepathy.

[SOURCE: Eddington Worst, p. 117] Charles and Camilla case: four of Camilla's personal planets fall into Charles's 12th house; she has all of them in Cancer (her own 12th house). He is the receptor — the one more consumed.

[SYNTHESIS — UPDATED DOCTRINE] The previous doctrine (second pass) scored 12th house overlays as simply less romantic than 7th/8th/5th. This needs refinement:

- 12th house overlays are NOT in the romantic tier (1, 5, 7, 8) but they are psychologically potent.
- They describe a bond that operates below consciousness — secret, compulsive, possibly transcendent.
- In a purely romantic report context, they should be flagged as intense but ambiguous, NOT as romantic positives.
- If the only notable overlays are 12th house, report should gently name the hidden-bond quality without calling it romantic compatibility.
- Report language: "This contact operates more in the hidden register than the romantic one — [Name B]'s presence tends to pull [Name A] inward, toward something that's hard to name openly."

---

### Stellium doctrine — proposed new pattern

[SOURCE: Eddington Worst, pp. 86–103] A natal stellium (3+ personal planets in one sign) requires a partner planet conjunct to the stellium sign for the relationship to feel emotionally resonant. Without this, "the synastry will likely consist of oppositions, squares, or low-impact aspects." Eddington's claim: "I firmly believe that a relationship without a conjunction to a partner's stellium will fail."

[SYNTHESIS] Implement as a detection pattern and informational flag — NOT a dealbreaker signal. Pattern name: `synastry.stellium_resonance.missing`. Trigger: Person A has 3+ personal planets (Sun, Moon, Mercury, Venus, Mars) in a single sign, and none of Person B's planets are within ~10° of that sign. Report: "A major cluster of [Name A]'s energy is in [Sign] — a partner with a planet there tends to feel this most strongly."

This is NOT a compatibility score element — it's a gap-to-be-aware-of, similar to an empty house in a natal chart.

**Exception case (Ann-Margret/Roger Smith, p. 103–104):** Ann-Margret had a 6-planet stellium in Taurus; Smith had no Taurus planets. Marriage lasted 50+ years. Resolution: mutual 7th house overlays compensated — Ann-Margret's stellium fell into Smith's 7th house, and Smith's Venus/Mercury fell into Ann-Margret's 7th house. The RECIPROCAL 7th house overlay overrode the stellium gap.

[SYNTHESIS] This is strong confirmation that house overlays can outweigh sign-conjunction gaps. Constellation's overlay scoring (especially for reciprocal 7th house) is directionally correct.

---

### Venus square Ascendant — new negative pattern

[SOURCE: Eddington Worst, pp. 69–72] "Venus finds beauty in the conjunction and ugliness in the square." The Venus person tends to dislike something about the Ascendant's physical presentation — appearance, walk, voice, posture, aesthetics, clothing. "The Ascendant person feels judged by Venus, and finds the Venus partner shallow." Creates push-pull dynamic: Venus is "both drawn to and repulsed by the Ascendant." Appears frequently in one-sided crushes. Not in Jung's marital aspect list at all.

[SYNTHESIS] This is a pattern Constellation does not currently detect but should consider for the overlay/angle contact layer (requires known birth times). Not a high-priority addition, but adds specificity when Ascendant contact data is available. Penalty (not dealbreaker) language: "There may be a subtle aesthetic friction here — something about [Name A]'s presentation that [Name B] hasn't quite reconciled."

---

### Sun conjunct Sun — revised scoring guidance

[SOURCE: Eddington Worst, pp. 73–85] Eddington confirms Jung's empirical finding: Sun conjunct Sun ranks #49 of 50 in most-observed marital aspects (i.e., almost never appears in lasting marriages). It creates "mirror camaraderie" — people feel they've found a soulmate based on similarity, but it's friendship energy not partnership energy.

[SYNTHESIS] Current Constellation scoring: unknown — but Sun conjunct Sun should score LOWER for romantic compatibility than it typically does in generic synastry tools. It's a friend signal. When it appears with no personal planets in the partner's romantic houses, gently note the "mirror" quality. Do not call it a soulmate indicator.

---

### Mirrored natal aspects in synastry — new nuance

[SOURCE: Eddington Worst, pp. 127–128] When an aspect in your natal chart (e.g., Venus opposite Saturn) is repeated in synastry, it creates "an instant comfort" for the person who has the natal aspect — but it "limits personal growth" for the other partner, who may "feel wrong a significant amount of the time."

[SYNTHESIS] Not currently implementable without natal chart comparison logic, but relevant to the report voice: when a challenging synastry pattern exists, acknowledge that one partner may experience it as "familiar territory" while the other experiences it as new friction. This nuances the "who feels this more" framing.

### Sargent-sourced

2. Venus–Saturn conjunction should include both a binding quality AND a conditional quality in report language — neither purely positive nor purely negative.

9. Intensity of attraction is not coded as depth of compatibility in any report output.
10. Venus–Neptune hard aspects should include language about idealization, not default to "deception" framing.

## Part 6 — Updated "What Not to Implement" List

(Additions to the seven from second-pass notes)

**8. Do not implement Wolff's four anima/animus archetypes as discrete patterns.**
Greene uses the Mother/Hetaira/Amazon/Medium framework to describe what men unconsciously seek. This cannot be deterministically derived from a natal chart (it requires interpretive judgment), and labeling someone's "type" as a pattern would be reductive and potentially offensive. Use as tonal background only.

**9. Do not implement "stellium compatibility score."**
Whether a partner has a conjunction to a stellium should be surfaced as informational context, not as a scored compatibility metric. A high-score stellium gap can be overridden by overlay architecture (Ann-Margret/Smith case). Present it as "worth knowing" without turning it into a number.

**10. Do not implement "mirrored aspect" detection in v1.**
Detecting that a natal aspect reiterates in synastry requires natal chart analysis beyond relationship analysis. Scope this to backlog.

**11. Do not call any overlay a "karmic connection."**
Eddington's 12th house material, Greene's anima/animus material, and Davison's overlay material all support the psychological reality of compulsive bonds — but "karmic" is un-falsifiable mystical language that the doctrine policy explicitly prohibits.

**12. Do not implement Jung's statistical marital aspect list as a scoring layer.**
Eddington cites Jung's empirical rankings as orienting context. Implementing them as scoring weights risks making the app feel like a compatibility calculator based on population statistics rather than an interpretive tool. Use the list to calibrate which aspects NOT to over-reward (Sun conjunct Sun, Venus conjunct Venus), not as a direct scoring input.

## Summary: What This Pass Adds

1. **Aspect-pair prose baseline** — Sargent fills the gap Arroyo identified: per-aspect interpretation text usable as the backbone of "How This Shows Up" report sections.

2. **Psychological depth layer** — Greene's anima/animus doctrine grounds the app's tonal stance: intensity of attraction ≠ depth of compatibility; falling in love involves projection; repair requires the individual, not just the couple.

3. **Penalty pattern calibration** — Eddington Worst establishes what currently over-scores (Sun conjunct Sun, Venus conjunct Venus as primary signals) and what may be under-penalized (Mercury opposite Mercury, 12th house overlay misclassified as romantic).

4. **New patterns to consider:**
   - `synastry.stellium_resonance.missing` (informational, not scored)
   - `synastry.angle_contact.venus_square_asc` (penalty, birth-time gated)
   - Revised 12th house overlay tier (ambiguous, not romantic)
   - Revised Sun conjunct Sun scoring (friendship, not marriage signal)

5. **Confirmed doctrine:**
   - Reciprocal 7th house overlay can override multiple negative patterns
   - Mars opposite Mars ≠ dealbreaker when overlays are strong
   - Water-earth Mars oppositions score worse than fire-air equivalents
