# Relationship Report Doctrine

## Purpose
The report must answer: **what matters most in this relationship field right now**.

## Anti-rule
**Do not write a glossary of planetary meanings. Interpret the relationship.**

## Prioritization rubric

### Tier 1 — Central signatures
- Synastry contacts to angles (Asc/Desc/MC/IC), especially Sun/Moon/Venus/Mars/Saturn/nodes.
- Luminary contacts (Sun-Moon, Moon-Moon, Sun-Sun, Moon-Venus, Sun-Venus).
- Venus/Mars activation to angles or luminaries.
- Saturn contacts to Sun/Moon/Venus/Asc/Desc.
- Nodal contacts to Sun/Moon/Venus/angles.
- Repeated overlays in 4th/5th/7th/8th/10th/12th.
- Composite luminaries/personal planets on angles (when reliable).

### Tier 2 — Relationship mechanics
- Mercury-Mars, Moon-Uranus, Venus-Saturn, Mars-Pluto, Venus-Pluto, Mars-Saturn.
- Composite Moon-Uranus, Mars-Pluto, Sun-Saturn.
- Meaningful overlays in 4th/7th/8th/12th.

### Tier 3 — Texture
- Broad or weakly activated aspects.
- Non-angular outer-planet contacts.
- Generic sign/house material without relational activation.

## Orb and exactness
- Exactness modifies strength **inside a tier**.
- Exactness does not override centrality.
- A very exact Tier 2/3 pattern should not outrank a close Tier 1 angular/luminary/Saturn/nodal signature.

## Trines and sextiles
- Do not overstate trines/sextiles as inherently “easy” or safe.
- Translate them into usable gifts and risks (e.g., bypass, complacency, avoidance of repair).

## House overlays
- Always name directionality (`A's Sun in B's 4th house`).
- Interpret overlays through relationship type context (ex/unresolved vs collaborator vs family).

## Composite interpretation
- Keep claims conservative when composite houses/angles are not fully implemented.
- If reliability is limited, centralize caveat in technical details and avoid over-claiming.

## Naming and directionality
- Use real names everywhere.
- Never ship user-facing `person_a`, `person_b`, or “the other’s house” phrasing.

## Uncertainty language
- Prefer: “may”, “can”, “tends to”, “if this is lived constructively”.
- Avoid deterministic verdicts.

## Output constraints
- Top signatures: 3–5 items max.
- Each signature must include:
  - directional technical phrase
  - plain-language strength
  - why-it-matters sentence
  - gift
  - care point
